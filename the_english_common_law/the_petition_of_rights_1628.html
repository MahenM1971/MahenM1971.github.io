The Petition of Rights (1628)

Having failed to intimidate Parliament, to raise taxes, to pay for his waning military budget, Charles I was forced to sign off on the Petition of Rights 1628, in return for Parliamentary support.

The Petition of Rights 1628, though not the first instance of a right of a habeas corpus in English law, nonetheless, enshrined the principal of habeas corpus in the English Common Law.

The Petition of Rights 1628, is a Constitutional document, forming part, and parcel of the unwritten Constitution of the United Kingdom.
